## Positive finding on a larger cold sample (the earlier alarm was partly thin-sample)

The cold-ECE degradation above rests on only ~105 cold IPOs. An **independent replication**
on the skworld Kaggle sample (546 OOS IPOs, **214 cold**; see `docs/REGIME_SKWORLD.md`)
found **cold ECE 0.083 — within the 0.10 tolerance**. So in ordinary corrections the
probability is more honest than our small sample suggested, and the current cold flag is,
if anything, **mildly conservative**. The flag stays as-is.

## Known limitation — deep cold / "IPO winter" (unvalidated; NOT acted on by design)

The model's edge appears **conditional on a functioning primary market**. There is one
historical IPO-winter in usable data — **2011–2013** — where, on the independent skworld
sample (**n = 54, 12 APPLYs, AUC ≈ 0.50, a *gross* listing-gain label ≠ our net-of-open
label, wide CI**), discrimination collapsed to chance: even heavily-subscribed IPOs stopped
reliably popping.

This is documented as a **caveat for the operator, not a feature**:

- The system is **not validated to detect** a sustained IPO-winter, and **deliberately does
  not auto-abstain** in deep cold. An auto-abstain rule would be a *second fitted threshold*
  ("market frozen") calibrated against **n ≈ 1 un-repeatable episode** — exactly the
  overfit-to-a-thin-slice we refuse everywhere else. There is no replication possible:
  the only earlier IPO bear (2008–09 GFC) predates usable subscription data.
- **Operator judgement applies:** in a prolonged primary-market freeze, treat *all* verdicts
  (including the probability and the ranking) as unreliable, regardless of what the system
  outputs. The cold flag (`market_regime ≤ −0.3`) addresses ordinary corrections only.

## Kaggle-data provenance (kept clean)

The skworld Kaggle set was used **only as an independent replication**, scored with its
**own gross listing-gain label**, and **never entered our calibrator** (mixing label
